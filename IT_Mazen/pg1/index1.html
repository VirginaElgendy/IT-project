The Time Machine


“I’ve had a most amazing time....”

So begins the Time Traveller’s astonishing firsthand account of his journey 800,000 years beyond
his own era—and the story that launched H.G. Wells’s successful career and earned him his
reputation as the father of science fiction. With a speculative leap that still fires the imagination,
Wells sends his brave explorer to face a future burdened with our greatest hopes...and our darkest
fears. A pull of the Time Machine’s lever propels him to the age of a slowly dying Earth. There he
discovers two bizarre races—the ethereal Eloi and the subterranean Morlocks—who not only
symbolize the duality of human nature, but offer a terrifying portrait of the men of tomorrow as well.
Published in 1895, this masterpiece of invention captivated readers on the threshold of a new
century. Thanks to Wells’s expert storytelling and provocative insight, The Time Machine will
continue to enthrall readers for generations to come.

Herbert George Wells

born September 21, 1866 in Kent , England - death August 13, 1946 in London , England , was a British novelist and short story writer. His most famous works include The Time Machine , The Invisible Man , The Island of Doctor Moreau , The War of the Worlds, and The First Men on the Moon . He is considered one of the founders of science fiction literature , and he gained his fame thanks to his novels that belong to that literary genre. About The Author


From the beginning, his novels were science fiction . His first novel,
published in 1895 and titled The Time Machine , enjoyed success among
readers. He published his second novel in 1896 , entitled “ The Island of
Doctor Moreau ,” about a mad scientist who turns animals into human beings.
In 1897, he published The Invisible Man, about a scientist who succeeds in
hiding himself, and The War of the Worlds was published in 1898, about a
Martian invasion of Earth. His novel The First Men on the Moon is considered
a foreshadowing of the methods of astronautship. Wells also wrote non-science
fiction novels, such as “Love and Mr. Lewisham” and “Keeps.” He also
published his book “Forecasts” in 1901. It was previously serialized in a
magazine under the title “An Experiment in Forecasting,” trying to predict
the state of the world in the year 2000. He succeeded in predicting some events.
